About Lex Consulting

Lex Consulting is an academic training and mentorship organization specializing in STEM Olympiad preparation, national and international competitions, and advanced enrichment programs for students who aim to excel in computer science, engineering, and mathematics.

Our team consists of experienced instructors, competition alumni, and curriculum designers who support schools and institutions worldwide. We provide structured training for logic Olympiads, ACSL, Technovation, robotics programs like VEX, AI fundamentals, and computational problem-solving.

Olympiad & Competition Programs

Logic Olympiad

International Logic Olympiad (ILO)

Global problem-solving in pure logic and reasoning.

The International Logic Olympiad gathers students worldwide to solve challenging logic puzzles and reasoning problems. Hosted in partnership with Stanford University, the contest expands access to advanced logic education, with Xprtis serving as the regional host for the East Asia & South Korea division.

  • Progressive rounds with puzzle-based and proof-style challenges.
  • Participation from 3,500+ students across 90+ countries.
  • Direct pathways into a Stanford-affiliated global logic community.
Girls in STEM

Technovation South Korea

App development and entrepreneurship for girls solving real-world problems.

Technovation South Korea invites girls to design mobile apps that address issues such as climate change, education, and mental health. Teams work with expert mentors to refine their ideas, build prototypes, and pitch their solutions on a global stage.

  • Team-based app projects focused on social impact.
  • Guidance in ideation, UI/UX, and pitch preparation.
  • Entry point into the worldwide Technovation Challenge community.
Computer Science

American Computer Science League (ACSL)

Foundational theory and algorithms for aspiring computer scientists.

The American Computer Science League (ACSL) builds computational thinking through written and online contests. Now in its 46th year, ACSL engages around 8,000 students worldwide in core CS concepts and problem-solving.

  • Covers recursion, number systems, data structures, and finite state machines.
  • Four seasonal contests followed by the ACSL Finals.
  • Supports a structured, multi-year CS competition pathway.
Robotics

VEX Robotics Competition

Hands-on robotics, game strategy, and engineering design in action.

VEX Robotics guides students through the full engineering cycle, from idea to competition-ready robot. Each season’s game encourages mechanical design, coding, and teamwork across a wide range of grade levels.

  • Defined roles for builders, programmers, and drivers.
  • Local, regional, and international VEX tournaments.
  • Strong alignment with engineering design standards and future STEM careers.
